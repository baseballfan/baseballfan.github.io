The Great Divide


Tour Info

Support

The journey is supported by two vehicles. One vehicle brings the luggage and kitchen equipment to the next accommodation, so that you don’t have to carry your luggage on your bike. The other vehicle provides lunch at the half-way stage and support on the road. Due to the strict American regulations on federal lands it is not allowed to support you on all stages with our second vehicle. Furthermore, it is not possible to give support on single tracks and on roads closed for motorized vehicles.

There are several sections through isolated forests and nature reserves of more than 60 miles where you are completely on your own. You won’t find shops, restaurants or other places to buy food or to refill your water bottles in these areas. We will make sure that you start these stages with a well-stocked camelbak with food and drinks.

We have a guide sweeping these sections, so that nobody has the feeling being out there alone. These are the stages that make The Great Divide an extremely tough bike expedition that requires strong physical and mental perseverance.

The journey is supported by a mechanic to keep your bike in perfect condition.>

Food

We provide a hearty breakfast (bread, cereals & fruit) and a tasty lunch at the half-way point on cycling days (on the few days where there is no vehicular access to the National Forest, we provide a pack lunch). In addition, we provide an extra meal at the end of each cycling stage (soup, bread, fruit, salty snacks) to ensure that you get everything you need to recover from a day "on the Great Divide". There is always sufficient drinking water and sport drinks in camp and at lunch.

Our chef prepares a healthy dinner in camp with the necessary carbohydrates to prepare you for the next stage of the adventure.


Accommodations

Most nights we will stay in well-equipped campsites and at several of these you will have the option to upgrade and rent a bungalow or caravan at your own expense.

We’ll stay for a minimum of 17 nights in motels during the tour. Hotel accommodation is included in Canmore on the night before we begin the tour and we provide hotel accommodation in Tucson, Arizona on the day we finish the tour in Antelope Wells, New Mexico.”


The Ride

Each participant can cycle the stages at his/her own pace. You can choose to cover the distance in a relaxed way with photo stops at every chosen moment or take a break in one of the few bars along the route. Alternatively, you can try to cover the distance as fast you can.
